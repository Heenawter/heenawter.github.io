Question #1

What genre should your video be?

The trending page of YouTube is broken down into 15 different categories. One possible subset of the more popular genres, in no particular order, is as follows:

  • Comedy
  • Gaming
  • Entertainment
  • Music
  • Howto & Style
  • Sports
With such a large variety of options to choose from, which category should you make content for? Obviously, the most obvious answer would be to make content for the genre you are passionate about and talented in. After all, if you are hopeleslly tonedeaf, it may not be worth your time to persue a career in music.

That being said, let us approach this problem as though each genre of video is equally likely and enjoyable. Considering this, what factors would influence our decision on which category to aim for?

Let's first consider the views each category gets (total).

As can be seen above, the Music category far outweighs the other genres in terms of overall views. In comparison to the second-most viewed category (Entertainment), Music has approximately 30-million more views; this is nearly double the number of views! With some thought, this result is fairly unsurprising. First off, music is universably shareable. This is especially true due to the lack of a language barrier when it comes to music in comparison to other forms of digital media. Although you may not understand the lyrics of a particular song, that by no means impacts your ability to enjoy it. As a result, it is not uncommon for music videos to trend in multiple countries. For example, here are just a few of the 55 different music videos that made the trending page of every country in our dataset:

In comparison, the next most universally shared category was Entertainment with only 29 videos that made every trending page, which is almost half of the number of music videos that met the same condition. The category 'Film & Animation' had 15 of these videos, and all the remaining categories had 10 or less. Of the fifteen possible categories, 3 of them had the minimum value of 1. This helps to illustrate our idea that music videos are more universally shareable in comparison to the other categories.

Music videos also have one of the highest replayability factors in comparison to the other categories. It is not uncommon for people without access to some other music streaming service to make use of YouTube's multitude of musical content. After all, this is the reason why the paid-for-subscription-service YouTube Red has an option to stream music in a manner not unlike Spotify. This is just another explanation for why this category dominates the other categories in terms of views.
